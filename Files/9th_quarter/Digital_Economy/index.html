Industry 4.0 • Agriculture • Data Science

📋
Project Overview

This project implements a real-time data pipeline to optimize agricultural irrigation through distributed temperature sensors deployed across farmland. The system leverages Industry 4.0 technologies to collect, process, and analyze sensor data, generating automatic alerts and controlling irrigation systems based on artificial intelligence and business rules.

🎯 Main Objective

Maximize water resource efficiency in agriculture through early detection of conditions requiring irrigation, reducing water waste and optimizing crop growth.

💡
Architecture Rationale

Why this architecture?

🚀 Streaming vs Batch

Crops require immediate response. Real-time processing prevents damage from drought or overwatering.

🔄 Apache Kafka

Handles multiple distributed sensors with high availability and fault tolerance. Scalable for hundreds of sensors.

📊 InfluxDB

Optimized for time-series with high write frequency. Fast queries for real-time alerts.

🧮 Spark Streaming

Distributed processing for complex analysis: moving averages, trend detection, data validation.

🎯 Smart Irrigation Logic

Simple Rules: If temperature > 30°C for 2+ consecutive hours → Activate irrigation

Advanced ML: Prediction based on historical patterns, considering time of day, season, and crop type

Optimization: Prevents unnecessary irrigation during rain and optimizes schedules based on evapotranspiration

Economic and Environmental Benefits:

  • • Water savings: 30-40% reduction in water consumption
  • • Yield improvement: Optimal irrigation increases productivity
  • • Cost reduction: Automation reduces labor costs
  • • Sustainability: Responsible use of natural resources
